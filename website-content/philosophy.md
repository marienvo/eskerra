# Philosophy

A thought doesn't know which device you happen to be holding.

Notes should fit how you actually think — in fragments, across devices, at odd hours, with the intent to return. Eskerra is built around that.

---

## Your notes follow your day

You think on the train. You write at your desk. You capture something half-asleep and finish it the next morning.

Eskerra makes that movement frictionless. Capture on mobile in seconds. Open the desktop and continue without setup, without waiting, without wondering where the note went. The vault is shared. The thread stays intact.

---

## Speed means presence

A tool that hesitates breaks the moment. A tool that opens instantly disappears into the background the right way.

Eskerra opens fast on mobile and desktop. Switching notes, searching the vault, getting back to where you were — all of it is quick, and stays quick. Not just on a new device on day one, but over time. Performance is treated as a core property, not something polished after everything else is done.

---

## Two tools, two modes

Most cross-platform apps treat every device as the same surface, just resized. The result is two mediocre experiences wearing the same coat.

Eskerra gives desktop and mobile genuinely different roles — because the moments are different.

**Mobile** is for the moment. Capturing an idea before it disappears. Reading a note on the way somewhere. Quick review before a meeting. The app opens fast and gets out of the way.

**Desktop** is for depth. Writing long-form, restructuring, editing, working through something over an hour. The editor is the center of the experience. Everything else is in service of that.

Write on mobile. Continue on desktop. The notes connect both.

---

## The interface improves. The notes stay

Editing Markdown tables by hand is tedious. Raw frontmatter is clunky. Eskerra builds better interfaces for these things over time.

But the notes on disk stay standard Markdown — readable in any editor, portable to any tool. What improves is the experience of working with the notes. What stays the same is the note itself.

---

## Software that behaves the way you expect

Eskerra opens notes the way you expect. It saves what you wrote. Notes from two years ago look like notes from today. Nothing is rewritten in the background; nothing accumulates silently.

A workspace that behaves consistently is one you can rely on without thinking about it. That reliability frees attention for the work.

---

## Built for the long run

The technology choices here are conservative on purpose.

Notes are plain Markdown. The vault is a folder. Sync is whatever works for you — Syncthing, cloud storage, a USB drive, or nothing. No subscription, no account, nothing in the system that stops working if Eskerra does.

Eskerra is designed to be worth using for years. And to let you leave without ceremony when you eventually move on.
